# Privacy Policy for Listenfy

Effective date: June 24, 2026

Listenfy is an offline-first local media manager for Android. It is designed to let you manage, play, edit, transfer, and back up your own audio and video library without creating an account and without telemetry.

This Privacy Policy explains what data Listenfy accesses, how that data is used, when data may leave your device, and how you can delete it.

## Developer and contact

App name: Listenfy  
Android package: `com.jv24dev.listenfy`  
Developer: JV24Dev / Listenfy project

For privacy questions, requests, or concerns, contact the developer through the GitHub repository:

<https://github.com/varasjona-24/Lisenfy-MVP/issues>

## Summary

Listenfy does not sell user data. Listenfy does not include advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or tracking SDKs. Most app data is stored locally on your device.

Some optional features can transfer data only when you intentionally use them, such as:

- importing media from a URL;
- sharing media with another Listenfy device;
- using Listenfy Connect on your local Wi-Fi network;
- uploading cookies or media to a backend you configure for advanced processing;
- opening external websites such as Ko-fi.

## Data Listenfy accesses or stores

### Local media files and metadata

Listenfy can access audio, video, and image files that you select or import. This may include file names, titles, artists, albums, covers, durations, lyrics, playlists, favorites, playback position, import history, and generated media variants.

Purpose: to build and manage your local media library, play media, edit metadata, show covers, organize playlists and collections, restore playback, and create backups.

Storage: locally on your device, unless you choose to export, share, transfer, or process files with an optional backend.

### Photos, images, and camera

Listenfy may use image access for covers, thumbnails, image crop/edit features, and local media organization. Listenfy may use the camera for QR scanning features such as pairing, transfer, or local connection flows.

Purpose: to scan QR codes, select/edit cover images, and support local media workflows.

Storage: selected images or generated thumbnails may be stored locally as part of your library.

### Bluetooth, nearby devices, Wi-Fi, and location-related permissions

Listenfy uses Bluetooth, nearby device, Wi-Fi, and location-related Android permissions to support local transfer, device discovery, Listenfy Connect, and playback/device routing features.

Purpose: to connect with nearby devices, transfer media locally, detect connection state, and allow browser-based control from devices on the same network.

Listenfy does not use these permissions for advertising, tracking, or background location tracking.

### Notifications

Listenfy may use notifications for media playback controls, import status, Listenfy Connect pairing/device events, timers, weekly summaries, and local recommendations.

Purpose: to provide expected app functionality and user-controlled reminders.

You can manage notification settings in the app and in Android system settings.

### Playback and recommendation activity

Listenfy may store local playback-related information such as recently played media, resume positions, listening events, favorites, skips, and recommendation history.

Purpose: to restore playback, build local recommendations, avoid repeating recommendations, and improve the in-app library experience.

Storage: locally on your device.

### Settings, backups, and app preferences

Listenfy stores app settings such as theme, playback preferences, equalizer settings, notification preferences, layout choices, import history, and backup/restore state.

Purpose: to keep your app preferences and restore your library setup.

Storage: locally on your device or inside backup ZIP files that you create.

### Cookies or backend tokens

If you choose to use optional URL/import or backend features, Listenfy may store or send configuration values such as a backend admin token or a `cookies.txt` file that you manually provide.

Purpose: to support user-initiated importing or backend processing features.

You should only provide cookies or backend tokens that you understand and control.

## When data may leave your device

Listenfy is local-first, but data may leave your device in these user-initiated situations:

1. **Media imports from URLs**: Listenfy may connect to remote websites or a configured backend to download or process media that you request.
2. **Optional backend processing**: if you use features such as 8D/spatial audio or instrumental/vocal generation with a backend, selected media and related metadata may be sent to that backend.
3. **Listenfy Connect**: when enabled, your device can expose a local web interface over your Wi-Fi network. Paired devices may receive playback status, queue information, selected media metadata, and streamed media content.
4. **Nearby/local transfers**: when you share media with another device, selected files and metadata are transferred to that device.
5. **Backups and exports**: when you create a backup ZIP or export files, the resulting files can be copied, shared, or stored wherever you choose.
6. **External links**: if you open Ko-fi or another external website, that website is governed by its own privacy policy.

Listenfy does not sell your data.

## Third-party services and libraries

Listenfy may open or interact with third-party services only as needed for selected features, such as:

- websites used for user-requested URL imports;
- optional backends configured by the user for processing;
- Ko-fi for voluntary donations;
- Android/Google platform services required by system features such as media playback, notifications, camera scanning, and nearby connectivity.

Third-party websites or services may have their own privacy practices.

## Data security

Listenfy stores most data locally on your device using app storage and files controlled by Android. Network communication is limited to app functionality such as user-requested imports, local network features, and optional backend processing. Listenfy does not intentionally transmit personal or sensitive user data for advertising or tracking.

Local network features such as Listenfy Connect should only be used on networks you trust.

## Data retention

Listenfy keeps local app data until you delete it, reset it, uninstall the app, clear Android app data, or remove generated files/backups.

Examples of retained local data include:

- library metadata;
- local media files and generated variants;
- playlists and collections;
- playback positions and recommendation history;
- settings and notification preferences;
- import history;
- backup files you create.

## Data deletion

You can delete Listenfy data by:

- deleting items, playlists, histories, generated variants, or backups inside the app where available;
- using app settings to reset preferences or clear storage-related data;
- deleting exported backup files from your device storage;
- using Android system settings to clear app data;
- uninstalling the app.

Listenfy does not require an account. If a future version adds accounts, this policy will be updated with account deletion instructions before release.

## Children

Listenfy is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.
